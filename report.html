import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Scatter plot: GDP per Capita vs Life Expectancy
plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))
plt.scatter(df['gdp_per_capita'], df['life_expectancy'], alpha=0.7)
plt.title("GDP per Capita vs Life Expectancy in 2022")
plt.xlabel("GDP per Capita (USD)")
plt.ylabel("Life Expectancy (years)")
plt.grid(True)
plt.show()

Figure 1: Scatter plot showing the relationship between GDP per Capita and Life Expectancy for various countries in 2022. Higher GDP per capita tends to correlate with higher life expectancy. Source: World Development Indicators.

# Bar chart: Unemployment Rate by Country
df[['country', 'unemployment_rate']].sort_values(by='unemployment_rate').plot(kind='barh', x='country', y='unemployment_rate', figsize=(10, 8))
plt.title("Unemployment Rate by Country (2022)")
plt.xlabel("Unemployment Rate (%)")
plt.show()

Figure 2: Bar chart showing the Unemployment Rate by Country in 2022. Countries exhibit a wide range of unemployment rates, reflecting differences in economic conditions. Source: World Development Indicators.

As shown in Figure 1, there is a positive correlation between GDP per capita and life expectancy. Figure 2 shows the unemployment rate by country, with significant variation across nations.

import plotly.express as px

# Create interactive scatter plot
fig = px.scatter(df, x='gdp_per_capita', y='life_expectancy', 
                 hover_name='country', 
                 title="Interactive GDP per Capita vs Life Expectancy")
fig.show()

As shown in Figure 1, there is a clear positive correlation between GDP per capita and life expectancy across countries. This suggests that wealthier nations tend to have better health outcomes.

The bar chart in Figure 2 highlights the variation in unemployment rates across different countries. Some nations experience much higher levels of unemployment, which may be indicative of underlying economic challenges.

According to the summary statistics in Table 1, the average life expectancy in the dataset is around 75 years, while GDP per capita varies significantly between countries, with some reaching as high as $42,000 USD.

The data used in this analysis comes from the World Bank (Bank 2022). According to economic growth theories (Doe 2021), countries with higher GDP per capita tend to have better health outcomes.
